SSAFY was my way out.
When I had wandered into a cave and could not find my way back out,
it was the lifeline that gave me another chance to take on a challenge.

At university, coding had been little more than an experimental tool that I explored without much depth.
Then I learned about machine learning and found it genuinely fascinating.
I prepared to attend graduate school in an AI-related field, but eventually lost my way.

My fundamentals fell far short of my interest,
and my passion was not strong enough to close that gap.

After spending so much time lost in that cave, I discovered SSAFY.
When I realized that my age was right at the upper limit of the eligibility requirements,
I thought, “The latest moment can still be the earliest moment,” and applied immediately.
The logic games in the entrance test were not as difficult as I expected.
I felt that my interview had gone poorly, but fortunately, I was accepted.

The first semester at SSAFY gave someone like me a solid foundation.
I had been able to solve the logic questions for admission, yet could not even implement rock-paper-scissors for the class placement test.
Now I could build a web page and make it work.
It amazed me that I had learned all of this so thoroughly in just four months.

I am a member of SSAFY's 13th cohort. That means twelve cohorts came before mine.
Backed by all of that experience, SSAFY has built a strong curriculum.
Looking back, we learned
algorithms, web development, back end, databases, front end, and finally the first-semester comprehensive project.
The scope was broad, but the learning was never shallow.

The AI curriculum was also updated quickly.
Along the way, good lectures and challenges helped me grow even further.
(The program even changed its name midway through the semester to include AI.)

For algorithms, we were able to take Samsung's Type A and Type B tests through SSAFY.
Unfortunately, I only earned the Type A certification.
Motivated by that disappointment, I achieved PCCP Programmers Level 4, and I plan to earn Type B in the second semester.

The atmosphere is another strength of SSAFY.
When you are surrounded by passionate people moving in the same direction,
their energy naturally motivates you and pulls you into that momentum.
You also build a valuable network and gain access to higher-quality information.
SSAFY supports study groups as well, so it feels like almost everyone participates in one.
I joined my first study group at SSAFY, and now I even run one myself.

My first goal after entering SSAFY was to achieve ALL PASS.
There were ten subject assessments and five monthly assessments, each graded PASS or FAIL. As soon as I heard that, I set the goal to keep myself working hard.
Fortunately, I completed every assessment successfully and even ranked first in one of the monthly assessments.
On top of that,

I placed second overall in academic performance for Daejeon Class 2 during the first semester.
I was a little disappointed not to finish first, but I had scored lower on about three tests,
and not earning the Algorithm Type B certification probably made a significant difference.
Still, I had exceeded my original goal, and I was incredibly proud.

The prize was a pair of Galaxy Buds3 Pro.
I had already been using Galaxy Buds Live, and the improvements were immediately noticeable.

The biggest difference was active noise cancellation.
On my previous earbuds, noise cancellation did little more than switch on or off.
The description of active noise cancellation said that it adjusts the level of cancellation according to sensitivity.
Thinking about it from a software perspective,
it seems to go beyond simply changing the cancellation level according to the volume of outside noise.
It appears to distinguish between more complex situations and respond differently to each case.
Calls were another major improvement. With my previous earbuds, audio would cut out or my words would be missed, making calls practically impossible, so I always removed them before answering.
When I tried a call with the Buds3 Pro, the call quality was clearly better.
Maybe it is because the microphones are closer to the mouth?
In any case, receiving such a great prize made the end of my first semester especially happy.

The second semester focuses on project-based, experiential learning.
I want to work with great teammates while making sure that I continue to grow as an individual.
I will keep giving my best throughout the remaining time
and make sure I achieve another great result.
